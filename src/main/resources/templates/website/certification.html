About Pavnam Certification

About Certification

Pavnam

The Pavnam Certificate is a trusted hallmark awarded to food business owners who meet our stringent standards for offering 100% pure vegetarian food. Through rigorous inspections and thorough evaluations, we ensure that certified establishments adhere to uncompromising standards of purity, hygiene, and contamination-free practices.


This certification guarantees that the food you enjoy from our certified vendors is prepared using only vegetarian ingredients, devoid of any cross-contamination, and crafted in a clean, sanitary environment. Pavnam Certification isn’t just a badge—it’s our promise of authenticity, integrity, and respect for vegetarian values.


By choosing Pavnam-certified establishments, you’re choosing peace of mind and the assurance that your meals align with the highest standards of vegetarian purity.

How does it work?

  • Fill the Application Form

  • Pricing & Review

    The pricing details will be provided once the application form is submitted, and each application will undergo a thorough review to ensure compliance with the standards of purity and hygiene.

    A thorough assessment of all documentation, results from the onsite audit, and compliance with the specified guidelines, ensuring everything is in order before approval.

Things to Keep in Mind

Terms and Conditions

• Eligibility: Food Safety Certificate is available to individuals or businesses involved in food handling, preparation, storage, and distribution within the food industry.

• Issuance of the Certificate: Upon successful completion of the required checks and examination, the Food Safety Certificate will be issued and The certificate is valid for a specific period (e.g., 1 or 3 years), after which it must be renewed.

• Responsibilities of Certificate Holders: The certificate holder is responsible for implementing food safety practices at all times during food production, storage, and sale.

• Renewal of Certificate: The Food Safety Certificate must be renewed before it expires to ensure continuous compliance with food safety regulations.

• Suspension or Revocation of the Certificate: The certifying body reserves the right to suspend or revoke a Food Safety Certificate if the holder fails to comply with food safety standards, regulations, or any terms of this agreement.

• Confidentiality: Any personal information provided during the application and certification process will be treated as confidential and will only be used for purposes related to the certification.

Guidelines

• Exclusion of Animal-Derived Ingredients: All forms of meat, including beef, chicken, lamb, and other types of animal flesh, must be excluded. The use of eggs (or egg-derived products) is not permitted in pure vegetarian food. Ingredients like animal-based stock, meat broths, or animal-based flavours.

• Vegetarian Sources of Ingredients: All ingredients must come from plant sources, such as vegetables, grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, fruits, and plant-based oils such as sunflower, soybean, olive, canola, or coconut oils. In the case of fermented products (e.g., bread, cheese), the enzymes or cultures used must be of plant origin. Animal-derived enzymes such as rennet (used in cheese-making) must not be used.

• Avoidance of Harmful Additives: Food additives such as gelatine, which is derived from animal collagen, and other animal-based stabilizers, emulsifiers, or colorants must be avoided. Examples include cochineal (a red dye) and carmine. Where needed, plant-based alternatives to emulsifiers, stabilizers, and colorants must be used. Common vegetarian substitutes include agar-agar (instead of gelatine) and plant-based emulsifiers.

• Equipment and Facility Standards: Preparation, processing, and manufacturing equipment used for pure vegetarian products should be dedicated exclusively to vegetarian food. Cross-contamination with non-vegetarian products must be strictly prevented.

• Raw Material Sourcing: All raw materials used in the production process must be verified to be free from animal-derived substances. It is essential to ensure that all raw materials, even if of plant origin, are free from contamination from non-vegetarian sources during transport, storage, or processing.

• Handling and Labelling: Products should be clearly labelled as “Pure Vegetarian” or “Vegetarian” and all ingredients, including additives, preservatives, and flavourings, should be transparently listed on the product label.
